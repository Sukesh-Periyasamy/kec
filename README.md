An **86-slide interactive presentation** designed to teach computer components through **engineering thinking**, not memorization. Every concept is immediately followed by a quiz that challenges understanding, not just recall.

> "Think like an engineer, not a spec reader."

- **HOW** parts interact as a system
- **WHEN** to choose one component over another
- **WHAT** actually matters vs. marketing hype

## ğŸ“š Content Overview

### Topics Covered

1. **Introduction** (Slides 1-6)
   - How computers really work
   - System thinking vs. individual parts

2. **CPU Architecture** (Slides 7-22)
   - What CPUs do
   - Cores vs. clock speed
   - Intel vs. AMD
   - Generations and naming

3. **Motherboards & Chipsets** (Slides 23-30)
   - CPU-motherboard relationship
   - Socket compatibility
   - Chipsets explained

4. **RAM & Memory** (Slides 31-40)
   - What is RAM?
   - Capacity vs. speed
   - DDR4 vs. DDR5
   - Dual channel concept

5. **Graphics Cards (GPU)** (Slides 41-50)
   - When you need a GPU
   - GPU types and specs
   - Power requirements

6. **Power Supplies** (Slides 51-58)
   - Why PSUs matter
   - Wattage myths
   - 80+ ratings
   - Cable management

7. **Storage Technology** (Slides 59-64)
   - HDD vs. SSD vs. NVMe
   - Speed comparisons
   - Performance impact

8. **Cooling Systems** (Slides 65-66)
   - Thermal throttling
   - Air vs. liquid

9. **Displays & Monitors** (Slides 67-70)
   - Resolution vs. refresh rate
   - HDMI vs. DisplayPort

10. **Ports & Connectivity** (Slides 71-74)
    - USB versions
    - Cable myths
    - Common issues

11. **Operating Systems** (Slides 75-78)
    - OS role
    - Driver importance

12. **BIOS & Optimization** (Slides 79-82)
    - BIOS basics
    - RAM overclocking (XMP/EXPO)
    - Laptop vs. desktop

13. **Final Thoughts** (Slides 83-86)
    - Engineering mindset
    - Course outcomes
    - Grand finale quiz
